Key Differences Between QlikView vs QlikSense

·         QlikView is most flexible business intelligence platform for changing raw data into knowledge. QlikView have the ability to write more advanced expressions. QlikView is the swiftest growing BI and data visualization tool, which is quick-to-deploy, easy-to-learn and perfect to get started with data visualization.

·         Qlik Sense’s build and use approach make it easy for you to create visualizations to extend your analytical capabilities and add extra value. You can generate reports and detailed dashboards instantly in QlikSense, it is a smart and user-friendly.

 

Similarities

·         Bottom Line: Both QlikView and Qlik Sense make it easy to explore and visualize data.

·         QlikView: QlikView offers two main levels of support: Basic and Enterprise. Basic customer support includes phone support during regular business hours, as well as a Knowledge Base, self-help guides, community forums, product updates, videos, webinars and training courses.

-          24/7 coverage for critical issues (also known as Severity 1 issues)

-          Coverage nine hours a day, seven days a week for less critical issues (Severity 2 and 3)

-          Business-critical service-level agreements prioritized to certified engineers or specialists

-          Custom product releases and upgrade recommendations based on the user’s needs

·         Qlik Sense: Qlik Sense also offers two-tiered customer service for Basic and Enterprise customers. Basic support is recommended for individuals or those with single-server installations. These customers have access to phone support during regular business hours, as well as online materials like the Knowledge Base, a self-help portal, forums, product updates, videos, webinars and training courses:

-          24/7 coverage for Severity 1 issues

-          Coverage nine hours a day, seven days a week for Severity 2 and 3 issues

-          Case handling by specialists or certified engineers

-          Release management, which offers recommendations on upgrades and product releases

·         Bottom Line: Both platforms offer Basic and Enterprise-level customer support tiers with similar features for each. Since all support is provided by the Qlik team, users can expect virtually the same level of support across both platforms. The primary decision is whether to choose the Basic or Enterprise level.

·         QlikView: The QlikView platform can be purchased through a combination of user-baed, server-based, document-based and application-based licensing.

-          Personal Edition: Free, featuring unlimited access

-          Enterprise Edition: Contact QlikView for pricing

·         Qlik Sense: Qlik Sense is available through four pricing plans: Desktop, Enterprise, Cloud Basic and Cloud Business.

-          Desktop: Free, featuring unlimited access

-          Enterprise: Available through user-based, server-based and subscription-based licensing options. Contact the Qlik Sense sales team for pricing.

-          Cloud Basic: Free, including sharing with up to five users

-          Cloud Business: $15 per user, per month (billed annually). This option is for groups and teams, offering comprehensive collaboration and analytics

Bottom Line: Both QlikView and Qlik Sense offer free plans for basic needs, which allows users to test out the software before making a business or enterprise-level purchase.
